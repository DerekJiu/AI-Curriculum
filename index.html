What Is This Curriculum?

"How Computers Learn" is a free, open-access curriculum that teaches artificial intelligence and machine learning concepts to elementary and middle school students (ages 9–12). Across 11 interactive lessons (~40 minutes each), students learn how AI recognizes images, understands language, makes recommendations, creates content, and can go wrong — all through hands-on activities using free online tools and Scratch programming.

No prior AI knowledge is required to teach this curriculum. Every lesson includes a complete slide deck with built-in activities, discussion prompts, and step-by-step instructions. Students need only basic computer skills (mouse, keyboard, web browser).

Quick-Start Guide for Educators

  1. 1

    Download the slide decks

    Download all 11 PPTX files from Google Drive. They open in PowerPoint, Google Slides, or Keynote. No special software required.

  2. 2

    Set up ML4Kids

    Go to machinelearningforkids.co.uk and create a free teacher account. Then create a "class" so students can log in without individual emails. This takes about 5 minutes. You'll need this starting from Lesson 3.

  3. 3

    Teach from the slides

    Each lesson is self-contained. The slides include all instructions, activities, discussion prompts, and step-by-step walkthroughs. You don't need any AI or programming knowledge. Just click through the slides and follow along with your students.

  4. 4

    Go in order (or pick and choose)

    The lessons build on each other, so teaching in order is ideal. If you only have time for a shorter program, this 5-lesson "essentials" track works well:

    Lesson 1 ML basics → Lesson 3 AI + Scratch → Lesson 6 Data quality → Lesson 9 Ethics → Lesson 11 Showcase
  5. 5

    Let students explore

    The best learning happens when students experiment, make mistakes, and try to "break" the AI. Encourage tinkering!
